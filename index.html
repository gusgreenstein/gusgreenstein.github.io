About Research

Gus Greenstein

Assistant Professor of Public Policy and Administration
Leiden University Institute of Public Administration
Faculty of Governance and Global Affairs

I am a political scientist specializing in environmental governance, public administration, and policy studies. My research employs both quantitative and qualitative methodologies, examining how organizational structures and management practices influence agency performance and institutional change within environmental and climate policy contexts.

My work focuses on policy implementation in public agencies, institutional change in environmental governance, deforestation control in Brazil, forest regulations in comparative international perspective, World Bank environmental policy, and development finance allocation at USAID.

I serve on the Steering Committee of the European Consortium for Political Research Standing Group on Environmental Politics, and am a Research Fellow of the Earth Systems Governance Project. Previously, I was a Research Fellow at SAIS, Johns Hopkins University. I have also consulted for the World Bank and the Netherlands Environmental Assessment Agency, and previously worked at The Brattle Group, International Rivers, and Conservation Strategy Fund. I am a Thomas Watson Fellow.

I hold a PhD from Stanford University's Emmett Interdisciplinary Program in Environment and Resources, an MPhil in Development Studies from Oxford, and a BA in Environmental Studies from Amherst College.
